# Provenance Guard — Planning

Provenance Guard classifies whether submitted text-based creative work was
written by a human or generated by AI, scores confidence in that classification,
surfaces a plain-language transparency label to readers, and gives creators a
path to appeal — behind rate limiting and a structured audit log.

## Overview

A single submission travels a fixed path: **submit → two independent signals →
combine into one confidence → attribution + label → audit log → response.**
Because perfect AI detection is unsolved, the system is designed to *communicate
uncertainty honestly* rather than force a binary verdict, and to fail toward
"uncertain" instead of accusing a human — a false positive (labeling human work
as AI) is the costly error on a writing platform.

## Architecture

```
SUBMISSION FLOW
  POST /submit {text, creator_id}
        │ raw text
        ├──────────────► Signal 1: LLM judge (Groq) ──► P_ai¹ ─┐
        │                                                       ├─► combine ──► confidence (P_ai)
        └──────────────► Signal 2: stylometric ─────► P_ai² ─┘        │
                                                                       ▼
                                          attribution (likely_ai / uncertain / likely_human)
                                                                       │
                                                                       ▼
                                          transparency label (reader-facing text)
                                                                       │
                              audit log  ◄── content_id, confidence, both signal scores, status
                                                                       │
                                                                       ▼
                                    response {content_id, attribution, confidence, label}

APPEAL FLOW
  POST /appeal {content_id, creator_reasoning}
        │
        ▼
  status ─► "under_review"  ──►  audit log (appeal record + original decision)  ──►  response {status, message}
```

**Submission flow:** raw text fans out to two independent detectors; their P(AI)
scores are folded into one calibrated confidence, mapped to an attribution
category and a reader-facing label, logged, and returned. **Appeal flow:** a
creator submits their reasoning for a `content_id`; the system sets the status to
`under_review`, appends an appeal record linked to the original decision, and
confirms receipt. No automated re-classification.

## Detection Signals

Two **genuinely independent** signals — one semantic, one structural — each
returning **P(AI-generated) in [0.0, 1.0]**.

### Signal 1 — LLM-as-judge (Groq, `llama-3.3-70b-versatile`)
- **Measures:** holistic semantic/stylistic coherence — "does this *read* as
  human or AI?" Catches generic register, uniform hedging, over-balanced
  argument structure, and stock AI phrasings ("It is important to note…").
- **Why it separates human/AI:** AI prose has a recognizable smoothed "voice";
  the model has seen enough of both to sense it.
- **Output:** a probability + one-line reason, parsed to a float score.
- **Blind spot:** unreliable as a detector — **biased against formal or
  non-native-English human writing**, foolable by lightly-edited AI, and can
  assert confidence it hasn't earned.

### Signal 2 — Stylometric heuristics (pure Python)
- **Measures:** structural statistics — **sentence-length variance**
  (burstiness), **type-token ratio** (vocabulary diversity), and **punctuation
  density**.
- **Why it separates human/AI:** AI text trends toward **uniformity** (even
  sentence lengths, moderate vocabulary); human writing is **burstier**, mixing
  short and long sentences and richer/rarer word choices.
- **Output:** raw metrics mapped to a single P(AI) score (mapping finalized in
  Milestone 4).
- **Blind spot:** **short texts produce meaningless statistics**; genre
  confounds (listicles, formal essays, and repetitive poems all look "uniform");
  it understands zero meaning and is gameable.

### Combination
`confidence = 0.6 · P_llm + 0.4 · P_stylometric` (normalized weighted average).
The LLM is the stronger holistic detector; stylometrics is an independent
structural cross-check. Weights live in `config.py` (`LLM_WEIGHT`,
`STYLOMETRIC_WEIGHT`).

## Uncertainty Representation

- **What the number means:** `confidence` is the system's estimated
  **probability the text is AI-generated**, a weighted blend of the two signals.
  A **0.6** means "leans AI, but inside the uncertain band" — surfaced to the
  reader as *inconclusive*, not as an AI verdict.
- **Mapping:** each signal outputs 0–1; combine via the 0.6/0.4 weighted
  average. (We do not fit a calibration curve — a known limitation; the weighting
  plus a wide uncertain band is our calibration.)
- **Thresholds (the "protect humans" stance):**

  | Condition | Attribution |
  | --- | --- |
  | `confidence >= 0.75` | `likely_ai` |
  | `0.40 < confidence < 0.75` | `uncertain` |
  | `confidence <= 0.40` | `likely_human` |

  The AI threshold is deliberately demanding and the uncertain band wide, so
  borderline formal-human text lands in `uncertain` rather than being accused.
  `0.51` (uncertain) and `0.95` (likely_ai) therefore produce different labels.

## Transparency Label Design

Reader-facing text; the confidence percentage is interpolated so different
scores read differently. `{ai}` = `round(confidence*100)`, `{human}` = `100 - {ai}`.

| Variant | Condition | Exact label text |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **High-confidence AI** | `confidence >= 0.75` | `⚠️ Likely AI-generated ({ai}% confidence). This text shows strong signals of AI authorship.` |
| **High-confidence human** | `confidence <= 0.40` | `✅ Likely human-written ({human}% confidence). No strong signals of AI authorship were found.` |
| **Uncertain** | in between | `❓ Attribution uncertain ({ai}% estimated AI). Our signals are mixed, so this result is inconclusive.` |

Design note: the AI label says "shows strong signals of" rather than accusing,
reflecting the false-positive asymmetry.

## Appeals Workflow

- **Who appeals:** the creator of a classified piece, referencing its
  `content_id`.
- **What they provide:** `content_id` + free-text `creator_reasoning` (e.g. "I
  wrote this myself; I'm a non-native English speaker and write formally").
- **What the system does:** looks up the original decision (`find_latest`),
  appends an appeal record to the audit log with `status: "under_review"`, the
  `appeal_reasoning`, and the original attribution/confidence, then returns a
  confirmation. **No automated re-classification.**
- **What a reviewer sees:** filtering the audit log (via `GET /log`) to
  `status == "under_review"` yields an appeal queue where each entry shows the
  creator's reasoning next to the original classification and both signal scores.

## Anticipated Edge Cases

1. **Repetitive, simple-vocabulary poetry** (e.g. a villanelle, nursery rhyme,
   or refrain-heavy poem). Deliberate repetition drives **type-token ratio down**
   and **sentence-length variance down** — exactly the stylometric fingerprint of
   AI uniformity — so Signal 2 misreads a legitimate human form as AI. Mitigated
   by the demanding 0.75 threshold and by Signal 1 potentially recognizing poetic
   intent, but this is a real weak spot.
2. **Very short submissions (< ~40 words).** Variance and TTR are statistically
   meaningless on tiny samples, so Signal 2 becomes noise and the combined score
   leans on the LLM alone — undermining the "two independent signals" premise.
3. *(Also anticipated)* **Non-native-English formal prose** — the core
   false-positive scenario, where both signals lean AI on register and
   uniformity — and **lightly-edited AI text**, where a human pass defeats both
   signals (false negative).
